Introduction

I approached this assignment with the brief in mind "Selling myself in 10 years", Using this I created multiple designs in photoshop for a basic website design and ended up landing on a mosaic style page using alot of white space for a minimal and abstract look. I felt that this design really stood out to me as in many of the personal projects I have made they focus heavily on the coding portion and being well designed of in the idea itself, instead of very well executed art, I tend to focus on the core and polish. Which I believe that this website represents my coding and design philosophy in all my projects.
I chose to not heavily use javascript as my skills lack in this area and also that a heavy use of java script in websites can usually endup harming the user experiance and making it a more convuluted process. I have seen this alot on website design for portfolios and these end up being difficult to navigate or having very bad performance whilst looking very nice.

Resource on portfolio creation

I relied upon previous knowledge from a past course I had taken with a concept artist and web designer (at my previous college), I didn't really rely upon anything on "How to create an online portfolio" I just used the resources above to figure out what is the best way to show off your work, I found that this is in 2 ways. For art is a gallary that is similar to artstations way of displaying art, and for more complex work such as games programming and games design. The best way to show this work off is through a blog style layout with articles images and then links to other areas of your work.

Planning

  • Very minimal use of colours and ensuring good readability by trying to minimize the amount of text on difficult to read colours and having black on white background usually for easily readable text
  • Large navigation and slighty larger than average fonts, with using a clear font to ensure readability and visibility of information
  • Use of bold, and different font sizes to show information and their importance to draw the eye.

I think I reached a good balance of keeping aesthestics throughout the website and good navigation, Instead of having the nav bar on all pages, it is only located on the main landing page which can be accessed by clicking onto my name at the top center of every webpage. I wanted to keep the website very neutral for accessbility reasons however, I need to talk to someone/research more about the abstract design being used for the background of the website, for now I have tried to keep as much text on a white background for clear visiblity for people with difficulties reading.
